Below is one approach to building a free effects chain in Reaper that can get you in the ballpark of BB King’s 1978 live tone. Remember, BB King’s sound wasn’t just one effect or a chain—it was his dynamic playing, the character of his guitar (“Lucille”), and the amp he used. Still, if you’re using a real plugged‐in guitar into Reaper, here’s a suggested chain with entirely free or bundled plugins that you can tweak to capture that warm, glassy, slightly overdriven blues tone:

1. Amp Simulation (Tone Foundation)

Use a free amp sim set to a warm, clean, tube‑amp sound that has a bit of natural breakup when pushed. Two popular choices are:

  • AmpliTube 5 CS by IK Multimedia (free version) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;or

  • LePou amp sims (there are a few free models available online)

Tip: Dial in a setting that mimics a vintage tube amp—you’re looking for plenty of warmth, a rich midrange, and just a whisper of saturation when you dig into the strings.

2. Subtle Overdrive (Slightly “Pushed” Tone)

To emulate the effect of a tube amp feeding into natural, overdriven tones when King really “opens up,” add a light overdrive pedal simulation. Look for:

  • A free Tube-Screamer–inspired overdrive plugin (if you can’t find a dedicated one, Reaper now comes with a few built-in saturation tools—experiment with those).

Tip: Keep the gain low so that you’re not turning your tone into modern high-gain distortion. The idea is to gently break up the signal for dynamic response.

3. Compression (Dynamic Control)

BB King’s playing is expressive and dynamic. Use a compressor to even out your tone slightly without squashing your natural playing nuances.

  • ReaComp (Reaper’s built‑in compressor) works great for this purpose.

Tip: Set a moderate ratio and slow attack so the initial “pluck” comes through, letting the natural dynamics of your playing shine through while controlling peaks.

4. EQ (Tone Shaping)

After amp simulation and overdrive, use an EQ to shape your tone. With blues tones, a slight emphasis on the mids can bring out the vocal quality of your guitar.

  • ReaEQ (Reaper’s native EQ) allows you to:

    • Boost the lower mids for warmth.

    • Enhance the midrange bite if needed.

    • Roll off any harsh highs.

Tip: Adjust subtly—BB King’s tone is all about nuance, so small tweeks go a long way.

5. Reverb (Live Ambience)

To replicate the feel of a live performance at the Bottom Line with its natural room ambience:

  • ReaVerbate (included in Reaper) or a free option like Voxengo OldSkoolVerb Set it to a short, room- or plate-style reverb so that you get depth without washing out your tone.

Tip: BB King’s live sound was intimate yet vibrant—avoid long, lush reverbs that might “drown” the clarity of your playing.
